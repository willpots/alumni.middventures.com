PCI

Since its inception in 2007, the Middlebury College Programs on Creativity and Innovation (PCI) has sought to supplement the traditional Liberal Arts curriculum with a series of programs for students to exercise their own creativity, innovation and entrepreneurial inclinations.

Meet the Alumni

Sydney Alfonso Middlebury '12

Sydney founded Etkie in 2012. Etkie is a jewelry and accessories company that creates handmade, unique pieces in collaboration with female artisans in developing areas. Sydney recently gave a TEDx talk in Hamburg Germany about her experience with entrepreneurship, and Etkie recently became funded by KIVA, a website that connects people to borrowers and partner institutions. Etkie will launch its crowd funding campaign in February on plumalley.co, which is exclusively formed for female entrepreneurs.

Shabana Basij-Rasikh Middlebury '11

Shabana is co-founder and president of SOLA (School of Leadership Afghanistan), a nonprofit that helps exceptional young Afghan women access education worldwide and jobs back home. SOLA is also the first, and perhaps the only, girls’ boarding school in Afghanistan.

Alex Bea Middlebury '12

Alex is an analyst at Voodoo Ventures in New Orleans, LA as a Venture for America Fellow. Voodoo Ventures plans to serve as an incubator for Internet start ups in the Gulf South, providing seed capital and working to develop business plans, business models, and develop software prototypes. Venture for America trains recent college graduates for jobs with startup comapanies.

Dave Campbell Middlebury '08

Dave founded GivingSomething in 2011. GivingSomething is an online donation platform enabling donors to send real goods to nonprofits. The site ships goods directly to the nonprofit’s address, encouraging transparency with donations to non-profits.

Anna Clements Middlebury '12.5

Anna co-founded Broad Street Maps, a consulting service that helps small health organizations design and implement mapping projects in an effort to strengthen project design and diversify communication strategies.

Doug Debold Middlebury '12

Doug is the co-founder and CFO of Perk Health, a startup based in Minneapolis, Minnesota. Perk works with companies to help people life more active lifestyles and promote happiness and healthiness. After a four month pilot, Perk’s tool has been fully adopted by health insurer Ucare for internal usage and will be rolling out to all 800+ employees by Jan 1.

Peter DiPrinzio Middlebury '13

Peter is currently working on a Venture for America fellowship with Pixelligent Technologies, an innovator in manufacturing high-quality nanocrystal dispersios and polymer nanocomposites. Peter works as a Business Analyst, overseeing Sales and operations (SOP) forecasting process and tools, gathering business intelligence and lead data, and analyzing market research reports.

Ariele Faber Middlebury '13

Ariele founded Cerebella Designs, a biologically-inspired fashion company. By designing patterns from microscopic images, the science lab quickly turns into an art studio, and the aesthetic experience that comes with specimen observation becomes more accessible to scientists and non-scientists alike.

Bianca Giaever Middlebury '12.5

Bianca is currently a filmmaker in residence at Adobe, making creative films on their software. She is also pursuing other freelance projects in film and radio. She lives in New York, NY.

Hannah Judge Middlebury '12.5

Ryan Kellett Middlebury '09

Ryan is a Deputy Editor focused on the Digital Audience at the Washington Post. He works to develop lasting news products and platforms with designers and developers that deepen on-site engagement through social media, UGC, and crowdsourcing and lead enterprise news efforts around crowd sourced stories.

Pier LaFarge Middlebury '10.5

Pier is a Co-Founder and the CEO at Spark, a startup focused on building a crowd investment platform that connects individual investors directly to small and medium scale energy efficiency projects.

Annie Makela Middlebury '11

In addition to teaching 2nd grade at Greenwich Country Day School, Annie helps lead the Aspen Ideas Festival at the Aspen Institute in Colorado. The Aspen Ideas Festival program of events includes discussions, seminars, panels, and tutorials from journalists, designers, innovators, politicians, diplomats, presidents, judges, musicians, artists, and writers.

Emily Nuñez Middlebury '12

Emily founded Sword & Plough, a social enterprise that creates veteran-made, fashionable and durable bags from recycled military fabrics and eco-leather accents. Her company donates 10% of profits to veteran initiatives such as the Wounded Warrior Project. In December 2013, S&P was named as one of the "6 Companies that give back all year" by FOX business. She is currently serving in the U.S. Army.

Corinne Prevot Middlebury '13

Corinne started Skida, a winter apparel company focused on hats and scarves, in 2007 and has since grown the business and moved her headquarters to Burlington, VT. During her time at Middlebury, she was named a Forbes All-Star student entrepreneur.

Katelyn Romanov '11.5

During her time at Middlebury, Katie helped the 2011 Solar Decathlon team finish 4th overall in their first every entry to the U.S. Solar Decathlon. She is currently working at Solar Mosaic. Mosaic connects investors to high quality solar projects. Their mission is to open up clean energy investing and fundamentally change the way energy is financed.

Astrid Schanz-Garbassi Middlebury '12

Astrid is working on a Venture for America fellowship with 121nexus as a Product Manager overseeing business and product development. 121nexus deploys cloud-based track & trace solutions for biomedical manufacturers.

Shane Scranton Middlebury '13

Post-graduation, Shane founded Lightwell in Middlebury, VT. Lightwell takes clients’ designs and turns them into computer-generated, three-dimensional images. Photo-realistic renderings improve communication among stakeholders by offering an unbiased visual representation of a proposed project.

Cloe Shasha Middlebury '12

Kara Shurmantine Middlebury '12

Kara is a Partnerships Manager at the Boston-based tech incubator, MassChallenge. MassChallenge, an annual $1M global startup competition and accelerator program, catalyzes the launch and success of high-growth, high-impact new entrepreneurs who we connect to the resources they need to launch and succeed immediately.

Ben Silton Middlebury '11

Ben currently is working as an Operations Analyst for Feed Resource Recovery. Founded in Boston, Mass. in 2007, Feed Resource Recovery has designed and implemented a groundbreaking Zero Waste solution for the food industry. Their innovative and proprietary approach leverages customers’ existing transportation and distribution systems to generate clean, sustainable power for onsite operations, reduce emissions and save millions of dollars on waste removal costs.

Brian Sirkia Middlebury '12.5

Brian co-founded RocketListings, a better craigslist used to network and track online buying and selling goods online, based out of Burlington, VT. Additionally, he serves a mentor and manager at the Vermont Venture Foundry. The Vermont Venture Foundry partners with early-stage companies to improve their business strategy and online presence.
